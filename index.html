About

The Issue

Climate change is a major issue that affects the world on many fronts, one of the most significant of which is agriculture. According to a document released by the OECD, not only will farmers face production issues, but resources will also become more scarce, leading to a cyclic pattern of harm. Moreover, the agriculture industry is one of the major contributors to climate change, both directly and indirectly. Therefore, it is important to consider agricultural practices when developing solutions and mitigations to the climate change issue (full article). This website was developed to help spur that movement by providing resources through which farmers can evaluate their carbon and water footprints and learn about ways they can implement in order to reduce the impacts of climate change.

The Program

The team behind this project participated in the Next Gen Coders Network, a program that connects students from Iraq, the Palestinian Territories, and the United States. Through this program, students learn about intercultural communication and software development. Over the course of 10 weeks, teams of 3-6 students design and build out a prototype website or application that addresses a global issue. This program highlights the importance of diverse voices and skills, especially when it comes to examining and addressing topics on an international level. The program is sponsored by the Stevens Initiative, which seeks to connect students in the United States and the Middle East and North Africa through virtual settings, and the U.S. Department of State. You can read more about the Next Gen Coders Network here, or the Stevens Initiative here.

Carbon emissions are a large contributor to global warming and climate change. According to Stephanie Osmanski, the effects of climate change include melting polar ice caps, rising sea levels, the destruction of natural habitats, and increasingly severe weather (full article). Human activities contribute greatly to increased carbon dioxide in the atmosphere. Examples of these activities include the use of oil, coal, and gas, as well as deforestation (full article). Currently, it is difficult to reduce CO2 emissions because of the global economy. For example, lower demand for a product causes prices to be lowered. However, low prices result in higher demand. Effects of Carbon Dioxide

Carbon dioxide is considered one of the harmful gases that affect human health, as continuous exposure to it leads to a series of side effects, and they are as follows:

  • Short-term effects. They occur when high levels of carbon dioxide are inhaled within a short time, which leads to suffocation (by replacing the air with carbon dioxide), unconsciousness, headache, vertigo and double vision, inability to concentrate, tinnitus, and convulsive seizures.
  • Long-term effects. They occur when carbon dioxide is inhaled in small amounts but regularly, which leads to changes in bone calcium or changes in the metabolism processes inside the body. Breathing carbon dioxide in large quantities can be life threatening, and if carbon dioxide gas is touches - when it is in its solid state - it may cause frostbite disease or the formation of blisters on the body.
